## CAMPAIGN ASSETS (Abbreviated)

### Value Prop (Summary)

Jobber is the #1 field service management software used by 250K+ home service pros. It replaces scattered tools (paper invoices, spreadsheets, phone tag) with one platform: scheduling, quoting, invoicing, CRM, automated follow-ups, and a client hub.

Key pain points addressed:
- Admin overload: owners spending nights doing paperwork instead of growing
- Missed revenue: no-shows, forgotten follow-ups, quotes that never convert
- Unprofessional image: handwritten invoices, no online booking, no review requests
- Scaling bottleneck: can not add crews without systems breaking

Social Proof Library:
- Average 37 percent revenue increase in first year
- Save 7+ hours per week on admin
- Michael Bedell: saved 20+ hours/month and $30K+/year
- One operator grew from $3.5K/day to $5-6K/day
- 40 percent business increase reported by multiple users
- 250K+ service pros, $13B+ in work processed
- Industry matches: landscaping, HVAC, plumbing, cleaning, electrical

### Approach 1: Here's the Math (Priority 1)
- Angle: Show how much time/money they are losing to manual admin
- Data required: employee_count, google_review_count (proxy for job volume)
- Research Required: NO
- Email 2 Mode: Expand/Reframe

### Approach 2: Insider Remark (Priority 2)
- Angle: Reference something specific the company is doing/achieving and connect it to a growth bottleneck Jobber solves
- Data required: none mandatory (research-dependent)
- Research Required: YES
- Email 2 Mode: 3 Specific Ideas

### Approach 3: Customer Empathy (Priority 3 — universal fallback)
- Angle: Acknowledge the daily grind of running a service business — quoting in the truck, invoicing at midnight
- Data required: none mandatory
- Research Required: NO
- Email 2 Mode: Expand/Reframe

### Lead List Context
Small home service business owners (plumbers, HVAC, landscapers, cleaners, electricians) with 2-20 employees who are still running operations on spreadsheets, paper, or basic tools. They are growing but hitting a ceiling because admin eats their evenings and they can not professionalize their client experience.

## BLINDSPOT CHECK

### Issues Identified During Simulation

1. **ADMIN HOURS PROXY ACCURACY**: The formula `employee_count x 1.2 = weekly admin hours` is rough. For Lead 1 with 8 employees, it produces 9.6 hours (rounded to "10"). This could be off — a well-organized 8-person crew might spend less, while a poorly organized one might spend much more. The email uses "probably spending around" to hedge.
   - SEVERITY: MEDIUM (same class of issue as review count proxy in Owner.com)
   - MITIGATION: Already handled by uncertainty language instruction in copywriter. The estimate is directionally useful even if imprecise — it makes the prospect think about their actual number.

2. **WEBSITE SCRAPE COST PER ROW**: The Website Scrape column now runs on EVERY row (even Math leads that have all the data they need). For a 1,000-lead campaign, that is 1,000 Claygent calls just for website scrapes.
   - SEVERITY: LOW
   - ACCEPT: The scrape is lightweight (visits 2-3 pages, outputs 50-100 words). The cost is marginal per call. The payoff is significant — Lead 1's Email 2 used the contact form insight, which it would not have had otherwise. The quality improvement justifies the cost.

3. **SUBJECT LINE CONSISTENCY AT SCALE**: With AI-generated subject lines, there is a risk of inconsistency. One lead might get "Admin Hours" while another similar lead gets "Crew Scheduling" — both are fine individually but mean A/B testing is harder because subject lines are not controlled.
   - SEVERITY: LOW (observation, not a bug)
   - NOTE: This is a feature-vs-control trade-off. Personalized subject lines should outperform static templates on average, but make it harder to isolate subject line performance in analytics. If the client needs controlled A/B testing on subject lines specifically, they could add a formula column that overrides the AI subject line with a static one for a percentage of rows.

4. **INSIDER REMARK LENGTH**: The insider remark "Going from solo to seven techs in four years is a serious pace" is 13 words — the SKILL.md specifies 3-6 words. This is too long.
   - SEVERITY: MEDIUM
   - FIX: The insider remark should be tighter. Something like "serious growth pace" (3 words) or "that is fast" (3 words). The full sentence works as a combined observable+remark, but the remark portion itself needs to be shorter. The copywriter prompt should reinforce the 3-6 word constraint for insider remarks.

5. **CALENDLY REFERENCE RISK**: The email says "replaces the Calendly and spreadsheet combo" — this assumes they are using spreadsheets. The website scrape found Calendly for booking but did not confirm spreadsheet usage. The copywriter inferred "spreadsheets" from the lack of a full CRM system.
   - SEVERITY: LOW-MEDIUM
   - MITIGATION: The copywriter should only reference tools confirmed by the website scrape or research. "Replaces Calendly with something built for field service" would be safer than assuming spreadsheets. Add guidance to the copywriter prompt: "Only reference specific tools you can confirm from {companyProductsServices} or {researchReport}."
